Running Analysis

Discover what your walk or run is telling us — and fix what's holding you back

The way you move tells a story. Small inefficiencies in how you walk or run can quietly accumulate into pain, fatigue, and injury over time — often in places you'd never connect to your movement. At Powerhouse PT in Laredo, Texas, our running analysis service uses detailed clinical assessment to reveal exactly what's happening in your movement patterns. Whether you're a runner dealing with recurring knee pain, someone recovering from a lower-limb injury, or an athlete chasing a personal best, a running analysis gives us the insight to build a plan that actually addresses the source.

Who Benefits From Running Analysis

  • Runners with recurring knee, hip, or foot pain
  • Athletes returning from lower-extremity injuries
  • People with chronic low back pain
  • Patients recovering from ankle sprains
  • Individuals with abnormal wear on footwear
  • Older adults with balance concerns
  • Post-surgical rehab patients (hip, knee, ankle)
  • Runners looking to improve efficiency and speed
  • Athletes with stress fractures or shin splints
  • Anyone experiencing unexplained leg or foot pain
  • Patients with neurological conditions affecting walking
  • Triathletes and endurance athletes

What We Assess

Walking Pattern Analysis

We evaluate your step length, stride symmetry, foot strike, and overall walking mechanics to pinpoint inefficiencies and compensations that may be contributing to pain or fatigue.

Running Form Assessment

For runners, we break down your form at different speeds — looking at cadence, ground contact time, vertical oscillation, and how your foot, knee, and hip work together on each stride.

Symmetry & Compensation Detection

We compare side-to-side differences in strength, mobility, and movement patterns — catching compensations that often develop silently after injury and drive future problems.

Foot & Ankle Mechanics

Excessive pronation, supination, and foot drop are common culprits behind knee, hip, and back pain. We evaluate how your feet interact with the ground and how that translates up the kinetic chain.

Hip & Core Stability

Weak hips and an unstable core are two of the most common contributors to inefficient gait. We assess how well your core and hip stabilizers are supporting your movement.

Personalized Correction Plan

Following the assessment, we deliver a targeted plan — including corrective exercises, manual therapy, cueing techniques, and training modifications — to address what we find.

What to Expect

01

History & Goals

We start by learning about your pain history, athletic background, and goals. Whether it's running a half marathon or just walking without pain, knowing your target shapes the entire assessment.

02

Static & Dynamic Assessment

We begin with a static posture and flexibility evaluation, then move into observing your actual walking and/or running patterns under clinical conditions.

03

Findings & Education

We walk you through exactly what we found — where your compensations are, why they developed, and how they connect to your symptoms. You leave understanding your body better than before.

04

Corrective Treatment Plan

We build a targeted treatment plan to address your movement deficits through a combination of manual therapy, corrective exercise, and movement re-training.
